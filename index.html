SkyDeal Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 Aug 2025

SkyDeal (“we”, “our”, “us”) helps users compare estimated flight prices across online travel agencies (OTAs) by combining publicly available portal fees with bank/card offers. This page explains what we collect, how we use it, and your choices.

What we collect

  • Search inputs: origin, destination, dates, passenger count, cabin, and similar parameters you enter.
  • Technical data: basic device/browser info, truncated IP for security/abuse prevention, and server logs.
  • Support contact: if you email us, we receive your email address and message.

What we do not collect

  • No payment card numbers or passport details.
  • No government IDs or sensitive categories of personal data.
  • No booking details—we do not issue tickets. Purchases happen on third-party OTAs.

How we use data

  • Provide flight search and price estimation features.
  • Operate, secure, and improve the service (diagnostics, preventing abuse, analytics).
  • Respond to your support requests.

Sharing

  • Service providers: hosting, analytics, error monitoring, and our flight data provider (e.g., Amadeus) as necessary to run SkyDeal.
  • No sale of personal data.

Retention

  • Search requests and server logs are typically retained up to 30 days.
  • Aggregated analytics (non-identifying) may be retained up to 12 months.

Security

We use encryption in transit (HTTPS), role-based access, and least-privilege practices. No system is 100% secure; please contact us if you suspect an issue.

Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or object to certain processing. To exercise these rights, contact us at youremail@example.com.

International transfers

We may process data in countries other than yours. We use reputable providers and safeguards where required by law.

Children

SkyDeal is not intended for children under 16. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes

If we make material changes, we will update this page and modify the “Last updated” date.
